Exhibit 3: Continuity

To what extent did the goals of the French Revolution change from 1789-1799?

The French Revolution’s goals progressed from demands for constitutional Monarchy to radical republicanism, and eventually dictatorship. Key Revolutionary ideas–such as centralized power, social hierarchy, and national unity–remained consistent throughout. Despite many changes in leadership and policy, the Revolution never fully achieved its original goals of liberty and equality for all.
